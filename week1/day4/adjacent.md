# **Word: adjacent**

## 1. Basic Meaning

**Adjacent** means **next to something** or **very close to something**.
(Ya kisi cheez ke bilkul paas ya saath ho)

---

## 2. Roman Urdu Meaning

* **Saath wala**
* **Bilkul paas**
* **Bagal mein**

Example:
“The library is adjacent to the lab.”
(Library lab ke bilkul saath hai)

---

## 3. Why This Word Matters

This word is common in:

* Maps
* Buildings
* Mathematics
* Graphs
* Computer Science
* Data Structures

It helps you explain **position** and **connection**.
(Yeh word cheezon ki jagah aur connection batata hai)

---

## 4. Daily-Life Usage

You can use it when talking about:

* Rooms
* Shops
* Seats
* Houses
* Roads

Example:
“My room is adjacent to the kitchen.”
(Mera kamra kitchen ke saath hai)

---

## 5. Academic Usage

In studies, this word is used in:

* Geometry
* Physics
* Research diagrams
* Maps
* Charts

Example:
“Two adjacent angles share one side.”
(Do angles ek side share karte hain)

---

## 6. Computer Science Usage

In Computer Science, **adjacent** is very important in:

* Graph Theory
* Networks
* Arrays
* Matrices
* Trees
* Algorithms

Example:
“Two nodes are adjacent if they are connected.”
(Agar nodes connected hon to woh adjacent hote hain)

---

## 7. Beginner Explanation

Imagine two houses standing side by side.
Those houses are **adjacent**.
(Yani dono ghar ek dusre ke bilkul paas hain)

In CS, if two nodes or cells touch or connect directly, they are called **adjacent**.
(CS mein jo cheezen directly connected hon unhein adjacent kehte hain)

---

## 8. Synonyms vs Similar Words

| Word            | Meaning                             |
| --------------- | ----------------------------------- |
| **Adjacent**    | Directly next to                    |
| **Nearby**      | Close, but not necessarily touching |
| **Neighboring** | In the same area                    |
| **Connected**   | Linked together                     |

Example:

* Adjacent rooms = side-by-side rooms
* Nearby shop = close shop, maybe not beside

---

## 9. Antonyms

* Distant
* Far
* Separate
* Remote

Example:
“The two buildings are far apart, not adjacent.”
(Dono buildings door hain, saath nahi)

---

---

## 12. Memory Technique

Think:

👉 **“Adjacent = Join + Near”**

If two things are joined closely, they are adjacent.
(Agar cheezen paas paas hon to adjacent yaad rakho)

---

## 13. Common Mistakes Students Make

### ❌ Mistake 1:

Using adjacent for something far away.

Wrong:

* “My school is adjacent to Lahore.” ❌

Correct:

* “My school is adjacent to the park.” ✅

---

### ❌ Mistake 2:

Confusing “nearby” with “adjacent”.

* Nearby = close
* Adjacent = directly next to
