## 📘 Word: **respectively**

---

## 1. Basic Meaning

* “Respectively” means: **in the same order**
* It connects two lists and shows which item matches which.

👉 Example idea:

* Ali and Sara got 80 and 90 marks **respectively**
  → Ali = 80, Sara = 90

---

## 2. Roman Urdu Meaning

* **“munazzam tor par”**
* **“har cheez apni order ke mutabiq”**
* **“same sequence ke sath”**

---

## 3. Why This Word Matters

* It saves time in writing
* It avoids repetition
* It clearly shows matching pairs

---

## 4. Daily-Life Usage

* Friends, sports, marks, shopping lists
* When matching names and numbers

Example:

* Hamid and Bilal bought pens and books **respectively**

---

## 5. Academic Usage

* Used in reports and assignments
* Used to compare data in order

Example:

* Physics and Chemistry marks were 70 and 85 **respectively**

---

## 6. Computer Science Usage

* Used in **data mapping**
* Used in **arrays, lists, outputs**
* Used in **algorithms and results**

Example idea:

* Input and output arrays match values **respectively**

---

## 7. Beginner Explanation

Think like this:

* Two lists = A and B
* “Respectively” = match A1 with B1, A2 with B2

👉 Simple rule:

* **First with first, second with second**

---

## 8. Synonyms vs Similar Words

* Similar:

  * “in order”
  * “correspondingly”
* BUT:

  * “respectively” is more precise

---

## 9. Antonyms

* randomly
* unordered
* mixed

---

## 10. Word Family

* respect (verb/noun base)
* respectful
* respectable
* respectively (adverb)

---

## 11. Common Phrases

* “A and B respectively”
* “X and Y are 10 and 20 respectively”
* “in that order respectively”

---

## 12. Memory Technique

👉 Trick:

* Think: **“First = First, Second = Second”**
* Roman Urdu:

  * **“pehla pehle se match, doosra doosre se match”**

---

## 13. Common Mistakes Students Make

* ❌ Using without two lists
* ❌ Wrong order matching
* ❌ Putting it in the middle incorrectly
* ❌ Overusing in simple sentences
